Child Safety Standards Against CSAE

Last updated: May 14, 2026. Applies to the AI Social application and this website.

AI Social has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sextortion, sexualization of minors, or any attempt to create, request, distribute, normalize, or facilitate this content or conduct.

What is prohibited

  • Creating, requesting, sharing, or promoting CSAM or sexual content involving minors.
  • Using the App to generate sexualized depictions of minors, even if synthetic, fictional, or AI-generated.
  • Grooming, coercion, sextortion, trafficking, or attempts to move a child into sexualized conversations or activity.
  • Links, instructions, code words, or other material intended to help people find, trade, or evade detection of CSAE content.
  • Any content that sexualizes children or appears to exploit a person who is under 18.

How AI Social is designed

AI Social is a simulated social feed. It does not provide public user profiles, direct messaging, media upload, or a project-operated social network backend. Content generation runs on your device or through an Ollama server that you configure. These design choices reduce opportunities for user-to-user abuse, but they do not permit unsafe or illegal use of the App.

Reporting concerns

If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. To report suspected online child sexual exploitation in the United States, contact the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children CyberTipline at https://report.cybertip.org/. Outside the United States, contact your local law enforcement agency or the appropriate national reporting hotline.

To report an AI Social app safety concern, use the repository's private vulnerability reporting flow at https://github.com/chartmann1590/AI-Social/security/advisories/new or the contact channels listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile. Do not post illegal content, CSAM, or personally identifying details about a child in public GitHub issues, discussions, or pull requests.

Our response

  • We review actionable safety reports and prioritize issues involving child safety.
  • We remove or reject project-hosted content that violates these standards when we control the location where it appears.
  • We may report suspected CSAE or CSAM to the appropriate authorities or child safety organizations where required or appropriate.
  • We may update prompts, app safeguards, documentation, or distribution listings to reduce misuse risk.

User responsibility

Users are responsible for how they configure models, prompts, and servers. You must comply with applicable law, model licenses, platform policies, and these standards. Do not use AI Social or any connected model provider to create, store, distribute, or seek CSAE content.
